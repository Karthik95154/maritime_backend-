<title> 
corrosion 
</title> 

<description> 
Corrosion is the loss of metal due to electrochemical action, especially in salt water environments:contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}. Ship hulls are highly susceptible to corrosion (rusting) because seawater creates an ideal environment for metal to oxidise:contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}. As corrosion progresses, plating thins and may perforate. Classification rules typically require replacement of hull plating when wastage exceeds ~15–25%【18†L37-L40】, since localized corrosion or buckling is not acceptable【18†L37-L40】. Causes include prolonged seawater exposure, trapped moisture, and damaged coatings. 
</description> 

<repair-process> 

- **Inspection and gauging:** Use ultrasonic thickness gauges to measure remaining plate thickness and decide if metal replacement is needed. If plating loss is moderate (e.g. up to ~15%), simple cleaning and recoating may suffice【18†L37-L40】. If wastage is higher or holes have formed, proceed to metal replacement. 
- **Surface preparation:** Remove rust and old coatings by abrasive blasting (e.g. with copper-slag or garnet) or power tools to expose clean metal. 
- **Cutting corroded steel:** Cut out corroded areas beyond the structurally sound metal (at least to 3/4 plate thickness)【35†L1098-L1103】. Make sure edges are smooth and rounded to avoid new stress concentrators. 
- **Fitting new plate:** Fabricate a new steel patch or insert plate (Grade A shipbuilding steel) to fit the cut-out area. Align it within one frame spacing if possible【35†L1098-L1103】. The new plate should match or exceed the original thickness. 
- **Welding:** Position the new plate and weld it continuously around the edges. Use low-hydrogen electrodes (e.g. E7018) and proper preheat (≈150°F) if required. Fillet weld inside and outside the hull if patch plate is used【4†L1138-L1147】. Ensure welds have full penetration and fair profiles. 
- **Finishing:** Grind and smooth weld seams. Inspect the repair (e.g. dye-penetrant or ultrasonic NDT) to confirm no defects. Apply primer and anti-corrosive paint (e.g. zinc-chromate primer followed by epoxy/polyurethane topcoats) to protect the repaired area:contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}. 

</repair-process>
