<title> 
fouling 
</title> 

<description> 
Fouling (biofouling) is the accumulation of marine organisms – such as barnacles, algae, and mussels – on the hull surface【24†L19-L27】. It increases hydrodynamic drag and fuel consumption. Ships use anti-fouling coatings (paints with biocides) to minimise this growth【47†L7-L11】, but over time fouling still occurs. Causes include biological attachment in calm waters and degraded anti-foul coating. Fouling removal is a regular maintenance task to restore performance. 
</description>

<repair-process> 

- **Inspection:** In drydock or alongside, inspect hull for fouling. Determine fouling severity and check coating condition underneath. 
- **Hull cleaning:** Remove fouling using divers or remotely-operated vehicles equipped with brushes or high-pressure water jets【24†L65-L73】. Earlier methods used divers with brush machines, but newer ROVs can jet-blast the hull efficiently【24†L65-L73】. Ensure the anti-fouling paint is not overly damaged during cleaning. 
- **Pressure washing:** After scrubbing, power-wash the hull to rinse off residual debris. 
- **Paint renewal:** If the anti-fouling coating is worn or abraded, apply a fresh coat. Typically, first apply a suitable primer if required, then spray or brush on anti-fouling paint formulated for marine use【47†L7-L11】. 
- **Finishing:** Allow the paint to cure as per specification. Return vessel to service promptly since clean hull greatly reduces fuel use. 
</repair-process>
